Dear Dr. ***PI LAST NAME***,

Your NSF OPP award ***NUMBER - TITLE*** is due to be completed in the new few months.  The USAP-DC exists to support NSF-funded researchers in fulfilling your data management obligations* and provides a central record of NSF-funded Antarctic projects and data. In preparation for your final report it is now time to ensure that your project is registered at USAP-DC and that all data from the award have been submitted to archives.

 

To register your project at   www.usap-dc.org  complete the webform at https://www.usap-dc.org/submit/project to be guided through the necessary steps to create a project page. For Collaborative Research projects, there should be only one project page - we recommend that the PI of the lead institution sets up the project page and can link the other awards during registration. Confirm with your program manager if you think that your award will not require a project page (e.g., workshop proposals).

 

Datasets can be added to the project either through submission for archiving at the USAP-DC or as links to external archives. To submit data to the USAP-DC follow this link https://www.usap-dc.org/submit/dataset to be guided through metadata creation and data upload.  We will use the information you provide to create an entry for your project in the Antarctic Master Database and send you the link to your AMD entry to include in your final report to NSF.  Project pages can also be created for past awards.

 

If you have any questions please reach out to us at info@usap-dc.org or see the help pages for project registration, the data archive and the FAQ.

 

Best Regards,

 

                            The USAP-DC Team
